    Introduction to email
    In order to send electronic mail over the Internet, we must be able to connect to a computer that has access to the Internet. We will send/read mail using MTSU's email server. Every registered student at MTSU is issued a student account on MTSU's email server. This is your permanent student account. If you have questions about your permanent student account, raise your hand to let the lab instructor know. (the Information Technology Division (ITD) in Cope 003 (898-5345) needs to be contacted.) We will use your permanent student account for email exercieses in this lab.

    Instead, during this lab you will be using the account id and password given to you for 1170. (We will usually refer to the account id as your username.) This course account is temporary and only lasts until the end of this class.

    Using your MTSU email account, you can send email messages to anyone who has access to the Internet. This means that you can send messages to users at MTSU and to users on other networks. To send email to another user at MTSU, you only need to know the username of the other user. To send email to a person who is not on MTSU’s network, you must know their complete email address.  

    Our objective in this section of Lab 1 is to practice e-mail applications. We will practice these applications by:

    • reading e-mail messages
    • replying to an e-mail message from your instructor, and
    • sending an e-mail message to your lab partner.

    Sending Mail
    Next we will learn how to compose a message and send it.

    Exercise 7:
    You will need a partner for this portion of the lab. Choose a partner (possibly the person sitting next to you) and write down your partner's username.

    1. Click on the Compose link in the left frame of the WebMail window. You should now see a screen similar to the screen that follows.  If you do not see a screen with this appearance, contact your lab instructor.

    2. Type in your partner's username (e-mail address) in the To:box.
    3. Type in your username in the Cc: box. This will send a copy of the message that you send to yourself. (This carbon copy will be printed in a later exercise).
    4. In the Subject: field, type in the subject of this message. Type in "Hello" as the subject and hit ENTER.
    5. You can now proceed to type in your mail message in the Message: field. Type a short message to your partner telling your name, your major, and your classification (freshman, sophomore, etc.) at MTSU.
    6. When you are finished, press the Send button to send the message. Click on the Continue button on the dialog box that appears.
    7. Practice sending a message one more time by sending your partner and the lab assistant (using an address the lab assistant will furnish, probably by having it written on the board) a second message. In this message tell them what your favorite hobby is
